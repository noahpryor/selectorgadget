The state of Vermont has 255 political units, or "places". This includes 237 towns, nine cities, five unincorporated areas, and four gores.

Unincorporated towns are those granted charters that the Vermont legislature later revoked due to lack of residents. While still technically towns, they have no local government; their affairs are managed by a state-appointed supervisor. They are Averill, Ferdinand, Glastenbury, Lewis, and Somerset.
